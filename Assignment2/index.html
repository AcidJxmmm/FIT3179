Lightning-sparked Forest Fires In USA (1992-2010)

Let's have a general view at the wildfire due to lighting in USA...

Wildfires are becoming a greater concern across the United States as a result of climate change, forest management, and an expanding wildland-urban interface.

Trends in wildfire activity, expenses, and destruction between 1992 and 2010 serve as an example of how the effects of wildfires are increasing. Over the duration of 18 years, it can be clearly seen that the wildfires do not only increase in number but also in size.

As to size of wildfire:

  • Class A - 1/4 acre or less.
  • Class B - more than 1/4 acre, but less than 10 acres.
  • Class C - 10 acres or more, but less than 100 acres.
  • Class D - 100 acres or more, but less than 300 acres.
  • Class E - 300 acres or more, but less than 1,000 acres.
  • Class F - 1,000 acres or more, but less than 5,000.
  • Class G - 5,000 acres or more.
